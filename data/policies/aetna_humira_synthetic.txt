Adalimumab (Humira) for Rheumatoid Arthritis
HCPCS J0135
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

COVERAGE RATIONALE

Adalimumab (Humira) is medically necessary for the treatment of moderately to
severely active rheumatoid arthritis when ALL of the following criteria are met:

1. The member is at least 18 years of age and has a confirmed diagnosis of
   rheumatoid arthritis (ICD-10 M05.x or M06.x) documented by a board-certified
   rheumatologist.

2. The member has had an inadequate response, intolerance, or contraindication
   to at least two conventional disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs)
   used for a minimum of three (3) months each. Acceptable conventional DMARDs
   include methotrexate, sulfasalazine, hydroxychloroquine, and leflunomide.

3. The member has had a tuberculosis (TB) screening test (interferon-gamma
   release assay or PPD) performed within the prior twelve (12) months and
   the result is negative, or the member has completed treatment for latent TB.

4. The member has documented active disease as evidenced by either:
   a. At least six (6) tender joints AND six (6) swollen joints on physical
      examination.
   b. Elevated inflammatory markers (CRP > 1.0 mg/dL or ESR > 28 mm/hr).

CONTRAINDICATIONS

Adalimumab is NOT covered when any of the following applies:

A. The member has an active serious infection, including active TB or
   sepsis.

B. The member has a history of demyelinating disease (multiple sclerosis,
   optic neuritis) or New York Heart Association class III or IV heart
   failure.

C. The member is pregnant and there is no documented discussion of risks
   and alternatives.

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

The following must be submitted with the prior authorization request:

i. Office notes from the prescribing rheumatologist documenting the joint
   examination and disease activity score.

ii. Records of prior DMARD trials including drug, dose, duration, and reason
    for discontinuation.

iii. Results of the most recent TB screening test.

iv. The proposed dosing regimen and duration of therapy.
