UnitedHealthcare Commercial Medical Policy
Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) of the Lumbar Spine
CPT Code: 72148, 72149, 72158
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

COVERAGE RATIONALE

MRI of the lumbar spine is medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria are met:

1. The member has had a documented neurologic examination performed by the
   ordering physician within the prior 30 days.

2. The member has completed at least six (6) weeks of physician-directed
   conservative therapy, including any combination of physical therapy,
   chiropractic care, anti-inflammatory medication, or activity modification,
   without clinically meaningful improvement.

   Exceptions: The conservative therapy requirement is waived when any of the
   following is documented:
   a. Progressive neurologic deficit (motor weakness, sensory loss, or reflex
      change) that has worsened over the most recent two-week period.
   b. Suspected cauda equina syndrome, including new-onset bowel or bladder
      dysfunction or saddle anesthesia.
   c. Known or suspected spinal infection, malignancy, or vertebral fracture
      based on imaging or clinical findings.

3. Plain radiographs of the lumbar spine have been performed and reviewed,
   unless an exception under criterion 2 applies.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

MRI of the lumbar spine is NOT covered when any of the following applies:

A. The member has a non-MRI-compatible cardiac pacemaker, implanted
   defibrillator, cochlear implant, or other ferromagnetic implant for which
   MRI is contraindicated by the device manufacturer.

B. The request is for screening of asymptomatic members.

C. The same study has been performed within the prior six (6) months and there
   has been no documented change in clinical status.

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

The following documentation must be submitted with the prior authorization
request:

i. Office notes documenting the neurologic examination and current symptoms,
   including duration and severity.

ii. Records of conservative therapy, including dates of service, type of
    therapy, and clinical response.

iii. Reports of any prior imaging of the lumbar spine performed within the
     past twelve (12) months.

iv. The clinical question that the requested MRI is intended to answer.
